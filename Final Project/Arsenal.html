The Gunners (North London)

The Arsenal Football Club, commonly known as Arsenal, is an English professional football club based in Holloway, North London. Arsenal compete in the Premier League, the top flight of English football. In domestic football, Arsenal has won 13 league titles (including one unbeaten title), a record 14 FA Cups, two League Cups, 17 FA Community Shields and a Football League Centenary Trophy. In European football, they have one European Cup Winners' Cup and one Inter-Cities Fairs Cup. In terms of trophies won, it is the third-most successful club in English football. The club has won 13 League titles, a record 13 FA Cups, two League Cups, the League Centenary Trophy, 15 FA Community Shields, one UEFA Cup Winners' Cup and one Inter-Cities Fairs Cup. It is also the only English club to go a 38-match league season unbeaten, receiving the nickname The Invincibles, and a special gold Premier League trophy.

HISTORY

The history of Arsenal Football Club from 1966 to the present day covers the third, fourth, and fifth periods of success in Arsenal's history, including three Doubles, a Cup Double, and success in European football, and an unbeaten league season. Following Bertie Mee's appointment in 1966, Arsenal won the Inter-Cities Fairs Cup, their first European trophy, in 1969-70, and their first League and FA Cup double in 1970-71. After stagnation in the 1980s, the return of former player George Graham as manager in 1986 brought a third period of glory. Arsenal won the League Cup in 1986-87, the Football League Centenary Trophy in 1988, two League title wins in 1988-89 and 1990-91, the FA Cup and League Cup double in 1992-93 and a second European trophy, the Cup Winners' Cup, in 1993-94. Arsenal's fifth period of success came with the appointment of Arsène Wenger in 1996. Under him, Arsenal won a second league and cup double in 1997-98 and then a third in 2001-02. In addition, the club were victorious in the 2002-03 and 2004-05 FA Cups, and won the Premier League in 2003-04 without losing a single match.

Kai Havertz

Kai Lukas Havertz (born 11 June 1999) is a German professional footballer who plays as an attacking midfielder or forward for Premier League club Arsenal and the Germany national team. Versatile forward Kai enjoyed a fantastic first season in red and white, getting stronger as the campaign progressed to finish with his best numbers to date: 20 goal involvements in the Premier League, with 13 goals and seven assists, plus one goal in Europe. Having initially featured in an advanced midfield role after arriving in the summer, the Germany international moved up front in the second half of the season and blossomed as a striker, with eight of his goals coming in his final 14 appearances of the season. Havertz has been described as a technically gifted, two-footed attacking midfielder who is comfortable with the ball on either foot and adept with his head. During his formative years, his style of play drew early comparisons to compatriot Mesut Özil, with Havertz himself admitting the midfielder was a player he looked up to. By the age of 19, and following numerous impressive performances in the Bundesliga, further comparisons had been drawn between Havertz and former Leverkusen players such as Michael Ballack and Toni Kroos and some began describing him as a combination of all three and an Alleskönner – a player who can do everything.

William Saliba

William Alain André Gabriel Saliba (born 24 March 2001) is a French professional footballer who plays as a centre-back for Premier League club Arsenal and the France national team. He is widely regarded as one of the best centre-backs in the world. France international William continued to establish himself as one of the best centre backs in England during his second season of first-team action. The elegant, pacey centre back became the first Arsenal outfield player in the Premier League era, and the first since Lee Dixon in 1989/90, to play every minute of the league season, and he was a cornerstone of the PL’s meanest defence – he won possession 213 times, more than any other centre back in the league. On 21 March 2022, Saliba received a call-up to France's senior squad for the first time as an injury replacement to Benjamin Pavard to play in friendlies against the Ivory Coast and South Africa. He played in five of France's 2022–23 UEFA Nations League games. In November 2022, Saliba was called up to the 26-man France squad that would compete in Qatar for the 2022 FIFA World Cup. He made his tournament debut on 30 November, replacing Raphaël Varane in the 63rd minute of France's final group game against Tunisia.

Aaron Ramsdale

Aaron Christopher Ramsdale (born 14 May 1998) is an English professional footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for Premier League club Arsenal and the England national team. Aaron Ramsdale enhanced his reputation as one of the best goalkeepers in the Premier League with a commanding season between the posts in 2022/23. He was on the pitch for every minute of our league campaign – the first player to do that for us since Wojciech Szczęsny in 2011/12 – and kept 14 clean sheets. Regularly producing some stunning saves when it really mattered, he won the Premier League Save of the Month award in both March (for his stop from Bournemouth’s Dango Ouattara) and April (from Liverpool’s Mohamed Salah). Ramsdale has represented England at all levels from under-18 to the senior team, and won the UEFA European Under-19 Championship in 2017. He was in the squad that finished as runners-up at both UEFA Euro 2020 and 2024.

David Raya

David Raya Martín (born 15 September 1995) is a Spanish professional footballer from Catalonia who plays as a goalkeeper for Premier League club Arsenal and the Spain national team. Experienced goalkeeper David strengthened his reputation as one of the best in Europe in 2023/24 after arriving on loan from Brentford last summer, with 16 clean sheets earning him the Premier League Golden Glove award. A sweeper-keeper, Raya "is renowned as a vocal keeper, as well as one who is adept with the ball at his feet". He "can play out from the back and is happy covering the space in behind", which allows a team "to play with a high line". As a result of his and Brentford's style of play during the 2020–21 season, Raya attempted 300 more passes than any other goalkeeper in the Championship. He was part of the Spain squad that won UEFA Euro 2024. He played one match, starting the final group stage match in a 1–0 win over Albania.
